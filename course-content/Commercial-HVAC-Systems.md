# Commercial HVAC Systems - Advanced Training
## Course Curriculum

**Course Duration:** 32 hours  
**Target Audience:** Experienced HVAC technicians, installers, supervisors  
**Prerequisites:** EPA 608 certification, 3+ years HVAC experience recommended  
**Certification:** Certificate of Completion in Commercial HVAC Systems  
**Career Path:** Commercial technician, supervisor, project manager

---

## COURSE OVERVIEW

This advanced course covers complex commercial HVAC systems including rooftop units, chillers, VAV systems, building automation, and energy management. Designed for technicians looking to specialize in commercial applications and advance their careers. Students will learn system design, installation, troubleshooting, and optimization of large-scale HVAC systems.

## MODULE 2: ROOFTOP PACKAGED UNITS (4 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand RTU design and operation
- Identify RTU components
- Perform RTU installation and commissioning
- Troubleshoot RTU problems

### Topics Covered

**RTU Fundamentals**
- All-in-one package design
- Heating and cooling in one unit
- Typical capacities: 3-25 tons
- Rooftop mounting advantages
- Easy replacement and maintenance
- Common manufacturers: Carrier, Trane, Lennox, York

**RTU Components**
- Compressor: scroll, reciprocating
- Condenser: air-cooled
- Evaporator: direct expansion
- Blower: variable or constant speed
- Gas furnace or electric heat
- Expansion device: TXV or fixed orifice
- Refrigerant circuit: R-410A typical
- Control board: electronic controls

**RTU Configurations**
- Heating options: gas, electric, heat pump
- Cooling only: summer operation
- Heat pump: heating and cooling
- Dual fuel: gas with electric backup
- Staged heating: multiple stages
- Variable speed: energy efficiency

**Installation Procedures**
- Roof preparation and structural support
- Vibration isolation: curb and pads
- Refrigerant line installation: insulation, slope
- Electrical connections: voltage, amperage
- Gas line installation: sizing, pressure
- Ductwork connections: sealing, insulation
- Condensate drainage: proper slope
- Startup and commissioning

**Commissioning Procedures**
- Electrical verification
- Gas pressure testing
- Refrigerant charge verification
- Airflow measurement
- Temperature differential check
- Control system verification
- Safety control testing
- Performance documentation

**Maintenance & Service**
- Filter replacement: 1-3 months
- Condenser cleaning: seasonal
- Refrigerant charge verification
- Electrical connections: tightness
- Gas furnace inspection: flame, heat exchanger
- Blower wheel cleaning
- Bearing lubrication
- Control system diagnostics

**Common RTU Problems**
- Low refrigerant charge: superheat too high
- High head pressure: condenser fouling
- Low airflow: filter clogged, blower issue
- Gas furnace won't ignite: safety lockout
- Compressor won't start: contactor issue
- Thermostat not responding: control issue

## MODULE 3: CHILLED WATER SYSTEMS (5 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand chiller operation and types
- Identify chiller components
- Perform chiller maintenance and troubleshooting
- Optimize chiller efficiency

### Topics Covered

**Chiller Fundamentals**
- Chills water instead of air
- Water circulates through building
- Larger capacity than rooftop units
- Typical range: 20-500+ tons
- Air-cooled vs. water-cooled condensers
- Centrifugal, screw, reciprocating compressors

**Air-Cooled Chillers**
- Condenser cooled by outdoor air
- Simpler than water-cooled
- Higher operating cost
- Easier installation
- Better for smaller buildings
- Typical capacity: 20-150 tons

**Water-Cooled Chillers**
- Condenser cooled by cooling tower water
- More efficient than air-cooled
- Requires cooling tower
- Requires water treatment
- Better for large buildings
- Typical capacity: 50-500+ tons

**Chiller Components**
- Compressor: centrifugal, screw, reciprocating
- Evaporator: shell-and-tube, plate-frame
- Condenser: shell-and-tube, plate-frame
- Expansion device: electronic, thermostatic
- Refrigerant circuit: R-134a, R-410A, R-123
- Oil management: critical for operation
- Purge unit: removes non-condensables

**Cooling Towers**
- Removes heat from condenser water
- Evaporative cooling principle
- Induced-draft vs. forced-draft
- Typical capacity: matches chiller
- Water treatment: critical
- Maintenance: cleaning, chemical treatment
- Legionella prevention

**Chilled Water Distribution**
- Primary loop: chiller to distribution
- Secondary loop: building zones
- Pump sizing and selection
- Pipe sizing: velocity considerations
- Insulation: prevents condensation
- Balancing: proper flow distribution
- Expansion tank: accommodates volume changes

**Chiller Operation**
- Startup procedures
- Load modulation: capacity control
- Entering and leaving water temperature
- Condenser water temperature
- Compressor staging
- Safety controls and interlocks
- Shutdown procedures

**Chiller Maintenance**
- Oil analysis: condition monitoring
- Refrigerant charge verification
- Condenser tube cleaning
- Evaporator tube cleaning
- Pump maintenance
- Valve operation
- Control system diagnostics
- Annual overhaul

**Chiller Efficiency Optimization**
- Condenser water temperature optimization
- Chilled water temperature optimization
- Load profile analysis
- Sequencing multiple chillers
- Energy management system integration
- Retrofit opportunities

**Common Chiller Problems**
- Low refrigerant charge: low evaporator pressure
- High head pressure: condenser fouling
- Compressor surge: low load operation
- Oil return problems: compressor damage risk
- Bearing wear: noise and vibration
- Tube fouling: reduced efficiency
- Pump cavitation: noise and damage

### Learning Objectives
- Understand VAV system design and operation
- Identify VAV components
- Troubleshoot VAV problems
- Optimize VAV performance

### Topics Covered

**VAV System Fundamentals**
- Variable Air Volume: changes airflow with demand
- More efficient than constant air volume
- Reduces energy consumption
- Improves comfort control
- Typical for large commercial buildings
- Requires sophisticated controls

**VAV System Components**
- Central air handler: large capacity
- VAV boxes: zone-level control
- Ductwork: main trunk and branches
- Dampers: control airflow
- Sensors: temperature, pressure, occupancy
- Control system: building automation system (BAS)
- Outdoor air intake: ventilation

**VAV Box Operation**
- Pressure-dependent boxes: simple, less efficient
- Pressure-independent boxes: constant flow
- Dual-duct boxes: heating and cooling
- Fan-powered boxes: boost fan for heating
- Thermostat control: zone temperature
- Minimum flow setting: ventilation requirement
- Reheat coil: heating in cooling mode

**Ductwork Design**
- Static pressure calculation
- Duct sizing: velocity method
- Friction loss: pressure drop
- Fittings: elbows, tees, transitions
- Balancing dampers: flow control
- Acoustic treatment: noise reduction
- Sealing: air leakage prevention

**Control Systems**
- Thermostat: zone temperature control
- Pressure sensor: duct static pressure
- Damper actuator: electric or pneumatic
- Reheat valve: heating control
- Building automation system: central control
- Occupancy sensors: demand-controlled ventilation
- CO2 sensors: ventilation optimization

**Air Handler Operation**
- Supply fan: constant or variable speed
- Return fan: exhaust air
- Outdoor air damper: ventilation control
- Filter: pressure drop monitoring
- Coils: heating and cooling
- Humidifier: moisture addition
- Dehumidifier: moisture removal

**Commissioning VAV Systems**
- Ductwork pressure testing
- Damper calibration
- Box flow verification
- Sensor calibration
- Control system programming
- Performance testing
- Documentation

**VAV Maintenance**
- Filter replacement: regular schedule
- Damper operation: smooth movement
- Actuator function: response time
- Sensor calibration: accuracy
- Coil cleaning: efficiency
- Fan bearing lubrication
- Control system diagnostics

**Common VAV Problems**
- Inadequate heating: undersized reheat
- Poor comfort: control lag
- High energy use: improper settings
- Noise: high velocity, ductwork
- Unbalanced flow: damper issues
- Sensor failure: control malfunction
- Actuator sticking: damper stuck

## MODULE 8: TROUBLESHOOTING & DIAGNOSTICS (3 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Apply systematic troubleshooting methodology
- Diagnose complex system problems
- Identify root causes
- Implement effective solutions

### Topics Covered

**Troubleshooting Methodology**
- Gather information: symptoms, history
- Observe: visual inspection
- Measure: gauges, meters, sensors
- Analyze: compare to normal values
- Hypothesize: possible causes
- Test: verify hypothesis
- Repair: fix root cause
- Verify: confirm solution

**Diagnostic Tools**
- Refrigerant gauges: pressure readings
- Multimeter: electrical testing
- Clamp meter: amperage draw
- Thermometer: temperature measurement
- Manometer: pressure drop
- Anemometer: airflow velocity
- Leak detector: refrigerant leaks
- Combustion analyzer: gas furnace

**Common Commercial Problems**
- Compressor not starting: electrical issue
- Low refrigerant charge: leaks
- High head pressure: condenser fouling
- Low evaporator pressure: airflow issue
- Inadequate cooling: multiple causes
- Inadequate heating: furnace or control
- Noise and vibration: mechanical issue
- Control system malfunction: sensor or logic

**Electrical Troubleshooting**
- No power: breaker, fuse, disconnect
- Contactor won't pull in: voltage issue
- Motor won't start: overload, capacitor
- Thermostat not responding: wiring, sensor
- Control board failure: replacement
- Sensor malfunction: calibration, replacement

**Refrigerant System Troubleshooting**
- Leak location: electronic detector
- Leak repair: proper procedure
- Refrigerant charge: proper amount
- Superheat/subcooling: proper values
- Compressor operation: normal sounds
- Valve operation: proper response

**Airflow Troubleshooting**
- Low airflow: filter, ductwork, fan
- Unbalanced flow: damper adjustment
- Ductwork leakage: sealing required
- Blower wheel fouling: cleaning
- Fan bearing wear: replacement
- Ductwork collapse: repair

**Control System Troubleshooting**
- Sensor calibration: accuracy
- Actuator response: smooth movement
- Network communication: connectivity
- Programming logic: correct operation
- Setpoint verification: proper value
- Fault codes: interpretation
