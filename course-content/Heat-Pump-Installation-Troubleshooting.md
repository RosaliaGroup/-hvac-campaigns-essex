## MODULE 1: HEAT PUMP FUNDAMENTALS (2 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand heat pump operation principles
- Explain heating and cooling cycles
- Identify heat pump types and applications
- Understand market demand and opportunities

### Topics Covered

**Heat Pump Basics**
- Definition: moves heat instead of generating it
- More efficient than resistance heating
- Heating and cooling in one system
- Reversing valve: changes refrigerant direction
- Coefficient of Performance (COP): efficiency metric
- Heating Seasonal Performance Factor (HSPF)
- Cooling Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER)

**Heating Cycle**
- Outdoor coil acts as evaporator (absorbs heat)
- Indoor coil acts as condenser (releases heat)
- Refrigerant circulates through reversing valve
- Heat from outdoor air transferred indoors
- Works even in cold weather (down to -15°F typical)
- Supplemental heat: electric resistance or gas

**Cooling Cycle**
- Outdoor coil acts as condenser (rejects heat)
- Indoor coil acts as evaporator (absorbs heat)
- Refrigerant circulates in normal direction
- Heat from indoor air transferred outdoors
- Same as air conditioner operation
- Reversing valve in neutral position

**Heat Pump Types**
- Air-source: most common, most affordable
- Ground-source (geothermal): most efficient
- Water-source: uses water body
- Hybrid: heat pump + gas furnace
- Mini-split: ductless systems
- Packaged: all-in-one unit

**Market Demand**
- Growing popularity: energy efficiency
- Government incentives: tax credits, rebates
- Environmental benefits: reduced emissions
- Cold climate heat pumps: new technology
- Retrofit opportunities: replace furnaces
- New construction: standard in many areas

**Efficiency Advantages**
- COP 3-4: produces 3-4 units of heat per unit of electricity
- Compared to electric resistance: COP 1
- Compared to gas furnace: 90-95% efficient
- Annual energy savings: 30-50% typical
- Payback period: 5-10 years typical

## MODULE 2: AIR-SOURCE HEAT PUMP SYSTEMS (4 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand air-source heat pump design
- Identify system components
- Perform installation and commissioning
- Troubleshoot common problems

### Topics Covered

**Air-Source Heat Pump Components**
- Compressor: scroll or reciprocating
- Outdoor coil: aluminum fins, copper tubing
- Indoor coil: similar to A/C evaporator
- Reversing valve: 4-way solenoid valve
- Expansion devices: TXV or fixed orifice
- Blower: variable or constant speed
- Backup heat: electric resistance or gas
- Refrigerant: R-410A, R-32, R-290 typical

**System Configurations**
- Split-system: outdoor and indoor units
- Packaged unit: all components in one
- Ductless mini-split: no ductwork
- Ducted mini-split: ductwork distribution
- Hybrid: heat pump + gas furnace
- Dual-fuel: automatic switching

**Heating Operation**
- Reversing valve energized: heating mode
- Outdoor coil acts as evaporator
- Indoor coil acts as condenser
- Refrigerant absorbs heat from outdoor air
- Heat delivered indoors via blower
- Defrost cycle: removes frost from outdoor coil
- Backup heat: engages when needed

**Cooling Operation**
- Reversing valve de-energized: cooling mode
- Outdoor coil acts as condenser
- Indoor coil acts as evaporator
- Refrigerant absorbs heat from indoor air
- Heat rejected outdoors
- Normal air conditioner operation

**Defrost Cycle**
- Frost buildup: reduces efficiency
- Defrost cycle: reverses to cooling mode
- Outdoor coil warms up: melts frost
- Backup heat: maintains indoor comfort
- Duration: 5-15 minutes typical
- Frequency: depends on conditions

**Backup Heat**
- Electric resistance: supplemental heating
- Gas furnace: hybrid system
- Engages when outdoor temp drops
- Improves comfort in extreme cold
- Increases energy consumption
- Reduces efficiency advantage

**Installation Procedures**
- Outdoor unit placement: proper clearance
- Refrigerant line routing: insulation, slope
- Electrical connections: proper sizing
- Ductwork connections: sealing
- Condensate drainage: proper slope
- Thermostat wiring: heating/cooling control
- Backup heat wiring: electric or gas
- Startup and commissioning

**Commissioning**
- Electrical verification: voltage, amperage
- Refrigerant charge: proper amount
- Superheat/subcooling: proper values
- Airflow measurement: CFM verification
- Temperature differential: heating and cooling
- Reversing valve operation: smooth switching
- Defrost cycle: proper operation
- Backup heat: proper engagement
- Control system: proper response

**Maintenance**
- Filter replacement: 1-3 months
- Outdoor coil cleaning: seasonal
- Refrigerant charge: annual verification
- Electrical connections: tightness
- Reversing valve: smooth operation
- Defrost sensor: proper function
- Thermostat: proper calibration

**Cold Climate Performance**
- Modern heat pumps: work to -15°F or lower
- Efficiency decreases: as temperature drops
- Backup heat: engages in extreme cold
- Proper sizing: important for cold climate
- Supplemental heating: may be necessary
- Payback period: longer in cold climates

**Common Air-Source Problems**
- Low refrigerant charge: high superheat
- Reversing valve stuck: won't switch modes
- Defrost cycle malfunction: frost buildup
- Backup heat won't engage: control issue
- Compressor won't start: electrical problem
- Noisy operation: refrigerant noise
- Poor heating: undersized system

## MODULE 3: GROUND-SOURCE (GEOTHERMAL) HEAT PUMPS (4 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Understand geothermal heat pump operation
- Identify ground loop configurations
- Perform geothermal system installation
- Troubleshoot geothermal problems

### Topics Covered

**Geothermal Fundamentals**
- Uses stable ground temperature
- More efficient than air-source
- COP 4-5: very high efficiency
- Requires ground loop installation
- Higher initial cost: lower operating cost
- Payback period: 7-10 years typical
- Excellent for new construction

**Ground Temperature**
- Stable below frost line
- Typically 45-55°F year-round
- Varies by location and depth
- Warmer in summer: heat rejection
- Cooler in winter: heat absorption
- More stable than air temperature

**Ground Loop Types**

**Closed-Loop Systems**
- Sealed loop: no water loss
- Antifreeze solution: prevents freezing
- Horizontal loops: shallow, requires space
- Vertical loops: deep, requires drilling
- Slinky loops: spiral configuration
- Pond loops: submerged in water

**Open-Loop Systems**
- Uses groundwater directly
- Requires adequate water supply
- Water returned to ground
- Very efficient: direct heat transfer
- Requires water quality testing
- Regulatory approval: may be required

**Loop Installation**
- Horizontal: trenches 4-6 feet deep
- Vertical: boreholes 100-400 feet deep
- Drilling: professional contractor
- Pipe sizing: based on flow rate
- Antifreeze concentration: proper mix
- Pressure testing: leak detection
- Flushing: remove debris

**Geothermal Heat Pump Components**
- Compressor: scroll or reciprocating
- Ground loop coil: heat exchanger
- Indoor coil: heating and cooling
- Reversing valve: mode switching
- Expansion device: TXV or fixed orifice
- Pump: circulates loop fluid
- Heat exchanger: transfers heat
- Backup heat: electric resistance

**System Operation**
- Heating: absorbs heat from ground
- Cooling: rejects heat to ground
- Year-round operation: stable efficiency
- Reversing valve: mode switching
- Loop pump: continuous circulation
- Thermostat: temperature control

**Heating Cycle**
- Loop fluid absorbs heat from ground
- Heat pump compressor increases temperature
- Indoor coil releases heat
- Backup heat: engages if needed
- Very efficient: stable ground temperature

**Cooling Cycle**
- Indoor coil absorbs heat from building
- Heat pump rejects heat to ground
- Loop fluid carries heat away
- Ground absorbs excess heat
- Very efficient: cool ground temperature

**Installation Procedures**
- Site evaluation: ground conditions
- Loop design: horizontal or vertical
- Drilling or trenching: ground loop
- Pipe installation: proper sizing
- Antifreeze mixing: correct concentration
- Pressure testing: leak detection
- Flushing: system cleaning
- Heat pump installation: indoor unit
- Ductwork: distribution system
- Electrical connections: proper sizing
- Thermostat: temperature control
- Startup and commissioning

**Commissioning**
- Loop pressure: proper operating range
- Loop temperature: heating and cooling
- Superheat/subcooling: proper values
- Airflow: CFM verification
- Temperature differential: heating and cooling
- Reversing valve: smooth switching
- Pump operation: proper flow rate
- Backup heat: proper engagement
- Control system: proper response

**Maintenance**
- Loop fluid: annual analysis
- Pump operation: smooth, no noise
- Pressure: within operating range
- Electrical connections: tightness
- Reversing valve: smooth operation
- Filter replacement: as needed
- Coil cleaning: annual

**Geothermal Advantages**
- Highest efficiency: COP 4-5
- Stable performance: year-round
- Lowest operating cost: long-term
- Environmental: zero emissions
- Quiet operation: no outdoor unit noise
- Space saving: no outdoor unit

**Geothermal Disadvantages**
- High initial cost: $15,000-$30,000+
- Ground loop installation: complex
- Site requirements: space or drilling
- Permitting: regulatory approval
- Payback period: 7-10 years
- Requires professional installation

**Common Geothermal Problems**
- Low loop temperature: inadequate loop
- High loop pressure: clogged loop
- Pump failure: replacement
- Antifreeze degradation: fluid change
- Reversing valve malfunction: mode switching
- Compressor issues: similar to air-source

## MODULE 5: HEAT PUMP TROUBLESHOOTING (3 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Apply systematic troubleshooting methodology
- Diagnose heat pump problems
- Identify root causes
- Implement effective solutions

### Topics Covered

**Heating Problems**

**Insufficient Heating**
- Low refrigerant charge: check superheat
- Reversing valve stuck: test solenoid
- Defrost cycle too frequent: sensor issue
- Backup heat not engaging: control issue
- Undersized system: capacity problem
- Poor insulation: building heat loss
- Thermostat setting: user error

**No Heating**
- Reversing valve failure: solenoid or valve
- Compressor not running: electrical issue
- Thermostat malfunction: wiring, sensor
- Backup heat only: reversing valve stuck
- Circuit breaker tripped: electrical fault
- Refrigerant leak: complete loss

**Cooling Problems**

**Insufficient Cooling**
- Low refrigerant charge: check superheat
- Condenser fouling: outdoor coil dirty
- Blower motor weak: reduced airflow
- Thermostat setting: user error
- Undersized system: capacity problem
- Ductwork leakage: reduced airflow

**No Cooling**
- Compressor not running: electrical issue
- Reversing valve stuck: heating mode
- Thermostat malfunction: wiring, sensor
- Refrigerant leak: complete loss
- Circuit breaker tripped: electrical fault
- Condenser fan not running: motor issue

**Defrost Problems**

**Frost Buildup**
- Defrost cycle not engaging: sensor issue
- Defrost cycle too short: timer issue
- Outdoor coil dirty: reduced airflow
- Low refrigerant: reduced efficiency
- Thermostat setting: continuous heating

**Defrost Cycle Issues**
- Too frequent: sensor malfunction
- Too long: backup heat running
- Won't engage: sensor or control
- Backup heat won't engage: control issue

**Refrigerant Problems**

**Low Refrigerant Charge**
- Symptoms: high superheat, low capacity
- Diagnosis: pressure readings, superheat
- Cause: leak or undercharge
- Solution: find leak, repair, recharge
- Prevention: proper evacuation, charging

**High Refrigerant Charge**
- Symptoms: high head pressure, high subcooling
- Diagnosis: pressure readings, subcooling
- Cause: overcharge
- Solution: recover excess, recharge properly
- Prevention: proper charging procedure

**Refrigerant Leaks**
- Detection: electronic detector
- Location: visual inspection, bubble solution
- Repair: solder or braze
- Testing: nitrogen pressure test
- Verification: electronic detector

**Electrical Problems**

**Compressor Won't Start**
- No power: check breaker, disconnect
- Contactor won't pull: voltage issue
- Overload tripped: reset, check amperage
- Capacitor failed: replacement
- Motor failure: replacement

**Thermostat Not Responding**
- No power: check breaker, wiring
- Sensor malfunction: replacement
- Control board failure: replacement
- Wiring issue: continuity testing
- Setpoint wrong: adjustment

**Reversing Valve Problems**

**Won't Switch Modes**
- Solenoid failure: replacement
- Pilot pressure issue: check pressures
- Valve stuck: heating only
- Wiring issue: continuity testing
- Control logic: programming error

**Noisy Operation**

**Compressor Noise**
- Liquid slugging: low charge
- Bearing wear: replacement
- Valve noise: normal operation
- Piston slap: normal in some units

**Refrigerant Noise**
- Hissing: normal, expansion device
- Gurgling: oil return issue
- Bubbling: low charge

**Mechanical Noise**
- Blower wheel: cleaning or replacement
- Bearing wear: lubrication or replacement
- Vibration: isolation pads
- Ductwork: rattle, sealing

**Performance Issues**

**Poor Efficiency**
- Low charge: reduce efficiency
- High head pressure: condenser fouling
- Defrost cycle: frequent cycling
- Backup heat: running too much
- Thermostat setting: too aggressive
- Building envelope: poor insulation

**Comfort Issues**
- Temperature swing: control lag
- Humidity: dehumidification issue
- Air distribution: ductwork problem
- Noise: system too loud
- Drafts: air leakage

**Troubleshooting Tools**
- Refrigerant gauges: pressure readings
- Thermometer: temperature measurement
- Multimeter: electrical testing
- Clamp meter: amperage draw
- Anemometer: airflow velocity
- Leak detector: refrigerant leaks
- Vacuum pump: evacuation

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Do I need EPA 608 certification?**  
A: Yes, this is a prerequisite for handling refrigerants.

**Q: What's the difference between air-source and geothermal?**  
A: Geothermal is more efficient but costs more to install. Air-source is more affordable but less efficient.

**Q: Can heat pumps work in cold climates?**  
A: Yes, modern cold-climate heat pumps work to -15°F or lower.

**Q: How much can I save with a heat pump?**  
A: Typically 30-70% energy savings compared to electric or gas heating.

**Q: What's the payback period?**  
A: Usually 5-10 years, depending on local energy costs and incentives.

**Q: Are heat pumps noisy?**  
A: Modern heat pumps are quiet, typically 70-80 dB.
