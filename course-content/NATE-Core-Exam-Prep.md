# NATE Core Exam Prep - Master HVAC Fundamentals
## Course Curriculum

**Course Duration:** 20 hours  
**Target Audience:** HVAC technicians preparing for NATE Core certification exam  
**Certification:** NATE Core Technician Certification (first step to full NATE certification)  
**Exam Format:** 50 multiple-choice questions  
**Passing Score:** 70% (35/50 questions)  
**Recommended Experience:** 2 years in HVAC field

---

## COURSE OVERVIEW

The NATE Core exam is the foundation of NATE certification and tests a technician's general knowledge, construction knowledge, and HVACR-specific knowledge. This course prepares technicians to pass the Core exam and is the first step toward full NATE Certification. After passing Core, technicians can pursue specialty exams (Installation, Service, or Senior) to earn full NATE Certification.

## MODULE 1: SAFETY FUNDAMENTALS (3 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Identify workplace hazards in HVAC environments
- Apply proper safety protocols and procedures
- Use personal protective equipment correctly
- Understand OSHA regulations and requirements
- Respond appropriately to emergencies

### Topics Covered

**Workplace Hazards**
- Electrical hazards: shock, arc flash, electrocution
- Mechanical hazards: rotating equipment, sharp edges, pinch points
- Chemical hazards: refrigerants, oils, solvents
- Thermal hazards: hot surfaces, cold refrigerant
- Pressure hazards: high-pressure systems, cylinders
- Height and fall hazards
- Confined space hazards

**Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**
- Hard hats and head protection
- Safety glasses and face shields
- Gloves: leather, nitrile, insulated
- Respirators: dust masks, cartridge respirators, SCBA
- Hearing protection
- Steel-toed boots
- High-visibility clothing
- Proper PPE selection for specific tasks

**OSHA Regulations**
- Lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures
- Electrical safety standards
- Fall protection requirements
- Confined space entry procedures
- Hazard communication (HazCom)
- Recordkeeping and incident reporting

**Emergency Procedures**
- First aid for electrical shock
- First aid for refrigerant exposure
- CPR and rescue breathing
- Emergency response procedures
- Evacuation procedures
- Incident reporting

**Safe Work Practices**
- Proper tool usage and maintenance
- Equipment operation procedures
- Ladder safety
- Scaffolding safety
- Electrical safety
- Refrigerant handling safety
- Pressure system safety

### Practice Questions
- What is the proper PPE for working with high-pressure refrigerant?
- What should you do if someone is exposed to refrigerant?
- Describe proper lockout/tagout procedures.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: How long is NATE Core certification valid?**  
A: 2 years. You must renew by completing 16 continuing education hours or retaking the exam.

**Q: Do I need NATE Core before taking specialty exams?**  
A: No, but most technicians take Core first as it covers fundamentals needed for specialty exams.

**Q: What if I fail the NATE exam?**  
A: You can retake the exam. Most testing organizations allow retakes after a waiting period.

**Q: Is NATE certification recognized nationwide?**  
A: Yes. NATE is the industry standard and recognized by employers across the country.

**Q: How does NATE certification help my career?**
